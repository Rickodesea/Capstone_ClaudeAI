DAMO 699:  Capstone Project  
 
Poster Presentation Guidelines 
The capstone poster presentation is the final stage of your applied analytics project. It is 
designed to simulate a professional research or industry showcase, where you 
communicate complex analytical work clearly, concisely, and visually. 
Your poster should demonstrate not only technical competence, but also clarity of 
reasoning, analytical rigor, and professional communication. 
 
1. Poster Size & Format Requirements 
• 
Recommended Size: 
o 36 inches (height) × 48 inches (width)  
o Landscape orientation is strongly recommended. 
• 
Alternative Acceptable Size (if printing constraints apply): 
o 24 inches × 36 inches (minimum acceptable size) 
• 
Format: 
o Printed, full-color poster or black and white poster  
o Professional layout (PowerPoint, Canva, Adobe Illustrator, or similar 
software) 
o Fonts must be readable from at least 3–4 feet away 
 
2. Required Poster Sections 
Your poster must include the following clearly labeled sections: 
1. Title, including:  
• 
Project title 
• 
Your name(s) 
• 
Course name 
2. Research Problem / objective of your 
capstone project 
• This will vary depending on 
your project, please consult 
with your supervisor if 
unsure.  

• 
Instructor name 
• 
Institution (students are free 
to use the UNF logo) 
• 
Date 
 
 
3. Research Question(s) and/or 
Hypotheses 
 
4. Data Description or preprocessing 
performed 
 
5. Analytical approach used (e.g., 
regression, classification, clustering, 
forecasting, etc.) 
• 
Justification for chosen method 
• 
Model diagnostics (if applicable) 
 
6. Key Findings 
• 
Major statistical results 
• 
Significant relationships 
• 
Model performance metrics 
• 
Clear interpretation (not just output 
screenshots) 
 
7. Visualizations 
• 
Graphs must be: 
o Clean 
o Properly labeled 
o Interpreted 
• 
Every chart must support a claim. 
 
8. Strategic / Practical Implications 
• 
What do the findings mean? 
• 
What should decision-makers do? 
 
9. Limitations & Future Research 
• 
Data limitations 
• 
Model limitations 
• 
Suggestions for future 
improvements 
 
 
 

Avoid overcrowding with raw output. Present summarized results visually. 
 
 
3. Design Expectations 
Your poster should: 
• 
Use consistent formatting 
• 
Avoid dense paragraphs 
• 
Use bullet points for clarity 
• 
Maintain visual balance (text vs visuals) 
• 
Use high contrast between background and text 
• 
Avoid excessive decorative elements 
• 
Think professional conference standard. 
 
4. Presentation Expectations 
• 
Each group will stand beside their poster. 
• 
You must be prepared to: 
o Explain your project clearly in 5–7 minutes 
o Answer questions  
o Defend your choices 
o Interpret results without reading directly from the poster 
• 
Professional attire is expected. 
 
5. Setup Requirement (Mandatory) 
All students must: 
• 
Arrive at least 30 minutes early on the day of the presentation 
• 
Use this time to: 

o Set up your poster 
o Ensure proper placement 
o Test any additional materials (if applicable) 
o Late arrival may impact your presentation grade. 
 
6. Assessment Criteria (High-Level Overview) 
Your poster presentation will be evaluated based on: 
• 
Analytical rigor 
• 
Clarity of research design 
• 
Proper use of data 
• 
Interpretation accuracy 
• 
Visual communication quality 
• 
Professionalism during Q&A 
• 
Overall coherence of the project
