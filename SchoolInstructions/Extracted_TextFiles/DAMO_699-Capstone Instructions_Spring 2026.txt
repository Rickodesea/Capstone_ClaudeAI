DAMO 699 - Capstone Project 
I. Capstone Project Overview 
Objective 
This capstone project is designed to synthesize and apply the advanced data analytics knowledge 
and skills developed throughout the Master of Data Analytics program. Working in teams of three 
to four students, you will simulate the role of data analytics consultants investigating and 
addressing a real-world problem within a selected industry, sector, or country. 
Projects may involve predictive analytics, statistical inference, machine learning, or exploratory 
data analysis, depending on the nature of the research problem and the available data. 
 
Learning Outcomes 
1. Analyze a real-world problem using appropriate data analytics methods. 
2. Select and implement analytical approaches suited to the project objectives. 
3. Apply the analytics lifecycle to initiate, plan, execute, monitor, and communicate a data 
analytics project. 
4. Use appropriate analytics terminology, tools, and techniques to design and implement a 
structured analytical investigation. 
 
Project Components 
Project Components and Instructions 
1. Team Formation and Topic Selection  
• 
Form a team of 3–4 students. 
• 
Select an industry, sector, country, or organizational context relevant to your project. 
• 
Identify a meaningful analytical problem or decision-making challenge that can be 
explored using data. 
 
2. Problem Definition and Analytical Framing 
• 
Clearly define the problem or analytical objective. 
• 
Develop research questions, analytical objectives, or predictive goals that guide the 
investigation. 

Examples may include: 
• 
explaining relationships between variables 
• 
predicting outcomes or trends 
• 
identifying patterns or segments 
• 
detecting anomalies 
• 
evaluating operational processes 
 
3. Data Collection and Preparation 
• 
Identify and collect appropriate datasets relevant to the analytical objective. 
• 
Clean, preprocess, and structure the data for analysis. 
If appropriate, students may: 
• 
develop a relational database using SQL 
• 
integrate data systems with visualization tools such as Power BI 
4. Analytical Methods 
Select and apply appropriate analytical techniques based on the project objective. These may 
include: 
• 
statistical analysis and inference 
• 
predictive modeling 
• 
machine learning methods 
• 
clustering or segmentation 
• 
simulation or optimization 
• 
anomaly detection 
• 
time-series analysis 
Students must justify why the selected methods are appropriate for the analytical problem. 
5. Diagnostics and Model Evaluation 
Where applicable, students should: 
• 
evaluate model performance 
• 
conduct diagnostic checks 
• 
assess robustness or validity of results 
• 
interpret analytical outputs appropriately 
6. Data Visualization and Communication 

Students must communicate their results clearly through: 
• 
charts and visualizations 
• 
dashboards 
• 
summary tables 
Visualizations should support analytical interpretation and decision-making. 
7. Final Report and Presentation 
Teams will compile their findings, analysis, and recommendations into a comprehensive final 
report. 
The report must clearly document: 
• 
the problem context 
• 
data sources 
• 
analytical approach 
• 
key findings 
• 
practical implications or recommendations 
Students will also deliver a professional presentation summarizing the project methodology and 
insights. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 

 
Proposal Section 
II. Capstone Project Proposal 
The proposal represents the initial project design stage and is due at the end of Week 2. 
The proposal should outline the analytical problem, proposed data sources, and planned 
analytical approach. 
Aim of Investigation  
Define the main objective of the project. 
Clearly state: 
• 
the analytical problem or decision-making issue 
• 
the research question, analytical objective, or predictive goal 
• 
the type of analysis you intend to conduct 
Examples may include: 
• 
identifying factors influencing an outcome 
• 
predicting a future trend 
• 
segmenting customers or users 
• 
detecting anomalies or inefficiencies 
• 
evaluating operational performance 
 
Research Question / Analytical Objective 
Clearly state the central question or objective guiding the project. 
Examples: 
• 
What factors are associated with customer churn? 
• 
Can machine learning models predict demand fluctuations? 
• 
What patterns exist in customer purchasing behavior 
 
 

Hypotheses (If Applicable) 
If the project involves statistical inference, teams may propose hypotheses. However, hypotheses 
are not required for projects focused on predictive modeling, machine learning, or exploratory 
analytics.

• 
machine learning
