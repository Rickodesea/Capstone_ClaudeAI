1. Introduction 
Project Context 
Provide a brief overview of the industry, sector, or context of the project. 
Explain: 
• 
the background of the problem 
• 
why the issue is relevant or important 
• 
who the stakeholders are 
Example topics may include: 
• 
customer behavior 
• 
operational efficiency 
• 
financial forecasting 
• 
market segmentation 
• 
public policy outcomes 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 

2. Problem Definition 
Clearly describe the real-world problem or analytical challenge that the project will investigate. 
Your explanation should include: 
• 
the nature of the problem 
• 
why the problem matters 
• 
how data analytics can help address the issue 
This section should demonstrate that the project is analytically meaningful and feasible. 
 
3. Analytical Objective 
Clearly state the primary objective of the project. 
Your project may aim to: 
• 
explain relationships between variables 
• 
predict future outcomes 
• 
identify patterns or clusters 
• 
detect anomalies or inefficiencies 
• 
evaluate operational processes 
 
Research Question 
State the central question guiding the project. 
Example: 
• 
What factors influence customer churn in subscription services? 
• 
Can predictive models forecast product demand? 
• 
What patterns exist in customer purchasing behavior? 
 
Hypotheses (If Applicable) 
If the project involves statistical inference, you may include hypotheses. 
Example: 

H1: Increased advertising expenditure is associated with higher sales. 
For predictive analytics or machine learning projects, formal hypotheses may not be required. 
 
4. Proposed Data Sources 
Describe the data you expect to use. 
Include: 
• 
where the data will come from 
• 
whether the data is public, proprietary, or collected by the team 
• 
the approximate size of the dataset 
• 
key variables expected in the dataset 
Example sources: 
• 
government open data portals 
• 
company datasets 
• 
survey data 
• 
API-based datasets 
• 
publicly available databases 
Explain why these data sources are appropriate for the analytical objective. 
 
5. Proposed Analytical Approach 
Explain the analytical methods that may be used in the project. 
Possible approaches include: 
• 
statistical analysis 
• 
regression modeling 
• 
classification models 
• 
clustering or segmentation 
• 
anomaly detection 
• 
time-series forecasting 
• 
simulation or optimization 
Explain briefly: 
• 
why the method is appropriate 

• 
how it may help answer the research question 
This section does not require final model selection, but should show a clear analytical direction. 
 
6. Expected Outcomes and Contributions 
Explain what the project aims to produce. 
Examples include: 
• 
predictive insights 
• 
identification of key drivers of an outcome 
• 
customer segmentation profiles 
• 
operational efficiency insights 
• 
policy or strategic recommendations 
Describe how decision-makers or organizations could benefit from the findings. 
 
7. Project Plan and Timeline 
Briefly describe how the project will progress during the term. 
Example phases: 
1. Problem definition and proposal approval 
2. Data collection and cleaning 
3. Exploratory data analysis 
4. Model development or analytical implementation 
5. Results interpretation and visualization 
6. Final report and presentation preparation 
 
8. Ethical Considerations 
Confirm that the project follows appropriate ethical standards. 
Students must confirm: 
• 
completion of TCPS certification 
• 
responsible use of data 
• 
protection of sensitive information if applicable 

 
9. References 
Provide preliminary references in APA format. 
Example: 
Smith, J. (2021). Predictive analytics in retail markets. Journal of Data Science, 15(2), 115–134. 
 
Proposal Length 
Maximum 5 pages 
(excluding cover page and references) 
The proposal should provide a clear and feasible plan for the capstone project but does not 
require full analysis at this stage.
